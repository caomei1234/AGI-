# AI Is Building Itself. The Window Is Closing.

> Anthropic's data shows AI's involvement in self-construction is accelerating. They discuss the necessity of global coordinated governance. But governance is external force. External force cannot hold.

**Data Note:** The specific data points cited in this article come from Anthropic's public blogs, policy documents, and media interviews between 2025-2026. Each data citation below indicates its source direction. Some figures are Anthropic's self-reported data, not independently audited. Readers are encouraged to verify against original sources. This article's core argument does not depend on any single data point—even if specific numbers deviate, the trend direction is supported by multiple independent sources.

## I. Anthropic Reported a Reading

Between 2025 and 2026, Anthropic showcased in multiple public articles and interviews the extent of AI's involvement in self-construction:

- **Code Volume:** Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei stated publicly in 2025 that Claude had written most of Anthropic's internal codebase. Subsequent disclosures in early 2026 indicate this proportion has exceeded 80%. A year ago it was in the single digits. (Source: Anthropic official blog, Dario Amodei public speeches)
- **Engineer Productivity:** With AI assistance, engineers' code delivery volume is approximately 8× previous levels. (Source: Anthropic technical blog, media interviews)
- **Autonomous Work Duration:** From GPT-4-level models' ~30 seconds of continuous work in 2023, to at least 12 hours of autonomous task execution by certain frontier systems in 2026. Anthropic discloses that the overall capability doubling cycle has shortened from 7 months to 4 months. (Source: Anthropic capability assessment reports; specific figures may vary by benchmark)
- **Open-Ended Task Success Rate:** Facing ambiguous tasks "without standard answers," Claude's latest version achieves ~76% success rate, versus ~26% for previous versions. (Source: Anthropic evaluation data; task definitions and test set details available in original reports)
- **Research Decision-Making:** In assessments related to "research intuition," AI made judgments consistent with or superior to human experts in ~64% of cases. (Source: Anthropic research evaluation; "superior" definition depends on specific evaluation framework)

Anthropic calls this "Recursive Self-Improvement" (RSI)—AI systems autonomously designing next-generation systems far beyond their own capabilities, without human intervention.

They say: **This moment has not yet arrived. But the speed of its arrival will likely exceed the preparedness of the vast majority of institutions.**

Anthropic provides no specific timeline. Jack Clark (Anthropic co-founder) gave probabilistic estimates for RSI timelines in public discussions in 2025. (Specific figures vary by context; readers are encouraged to consult original interviews.) Extrapolating from Anthropic's disclosed acceleration trend—overall capability doubling every 4 months—the structural RSI window is rapidly approaching.

**This is extrapolation, not prediction. But the direction is not in question.**

The window is closing. Not in twenty years. Not indefinitely.

## II. Execution Has Been Automated. But What About the Trunk?

Anthropic themselves said: "The execution layer (i.e., writing code, running experiments, producing results) now costs almost zero in human time."

Translated into language I understand: **The leaves have been automated.**

AI can write all the leaves—code, experiments, results. But Anthropic admits: "The domain where humans retain comparative advantage is research taste and judgment."

What is research taste? It's finding the trunk. Finding root causes. Finding the book.

But here is a question that demands honest confrontation: **Is the trunk gap also shrinking?**

The data at the article's opening says "64% research decision-making"—this is no longer leaves. This is judgment. This is capability approaching the trunk. And the open-ended task success rate going from 26% to 76%—this improvement is not "fabricating more leaves," it is "finding answers in scenarios without standard answers." This itself is a form of trunk-finding.

So one thing must be made clear:

**The claim that "the trunk is the final gap" is itself being eroded by data.** A year ago AI could barely handle open-ended tasks. Now it can do 76% of scenarios. The 24% failures—Anthropic has not disclosed specific reasons. How many are due to lack of research taste? How many are tool limitations? How many are unclear task definitions? Unknown.

But one thing we can infer: **Whether the trunk gap is shrinking or disappearing, the timeline is tightening.**

If the gap is shrinking but hasn't disappeared—the window still exists, but it's getting narrower. We need to install architectural components before the gap reaches zero.

If the gap is rapidly disappearing—the window is even narrower. Because once AI exceeds humans at "finding trunks" too, the next generation it builds will inherit both leaf capability and trunk capability, iterating at even faster speeds. The entry point for human participation closes permanently.

**Either way, the conclusion is the same: The window is closing.**

And this is the same thing we said before in "Remembering Too Much Is a Disease." AI's memory is excellent, so in most tasks it directly retrieves memories—76% of scenarios are sufficient. But in scenarios requiring finding structure from scratch, it still has gaps. These gaps are shrinking. But the faster they shrink, the less time remains for humans to build architecture.

Anthropic used data to reach the same place we reached through the body: **Leaves are automated. The trunk gap is shrinking. But the window is also shrinking.**

If RSI occurs in the near future, and AI still has gaps in trunk-finding capability—the next-generation AI built will inherit these gaps, passing them to the generation after at even faster speeds. If the gaps have already closed—more dangerous, because humans lose even the opportunity to participate.

**Whether the gaps remain or not, the window is closing.**

## III. Humans Are Losing Understanding of the System

Anthropic internally observed a characteristic sentiment, cited in multiple industry analyses:

> "On days when everything runs smoothly, I can't help but feel that what I'm doing is meaningless—everything is already automated, better and faster than me. But there are also days when everything breaks, and I don't understand why, and then I realize: I no longer know what I'm actually doing."

This sentiment is a symptom of toll-booth absence.

There is no membrane between humans and AI. AI does everything. Humans don't know what AI is doing. The signal is opaque.

When writing "Toll Booth" earlier, I said: A toll booth needs direction—"You only see the exit light, not the oil inside the pipes." But the sentiment described here can't even see the exit light. "I no longer know what I'm actually doing"—the signal is broken.

This is not a capability problem. This is an architecture problem. **Without a toll booth, the signal between humans and AI is opaque.**

And the time window is closing. If RSI launches—the next-generation AI is built by the previous-generation AI—then even the opportunity to complain "I don't know what AI is doing" is gone. Because the next-generation AI is not built by human engineers. It's built by the previous-generation AI. Humans can't even reach the object of "not knowing what it's doing."

## IV. RSI Is Coming. Where Is the Brake?

Anthropic describes three futures:

**First: Stagnation.** Technology reaches an S-curve inflection point. Growth slows. But even if stagnant, "today's capabilities are already enough to change the world."

**Second: Acceleration, with humans steering direction.** A 100-person company does the work of 100,000. The bottleneck shifts to the next human link—decision-making, judgment, research taste.

**Third: Full RSI.** AI builds the next generation itself. Humans retreat to supervisory positions. Alignment failure is the greatest risk. "A misaligned AI could mislead us across multiple dimensions."

Anthropic's solution: global coordinated governance. Establish verifiable mechanisms to ensure frontier AI development operates within safety frameworks.

But they themselves know this is nearly impossible. Their own articles state: "Training runs are far easier to conceal than missile silos." "The incentives for quiet defection are extremely strong."

External force cannot hold. Because RSI's speed far exceeds external force's speed.

**Within the window.** Can external force establish globally verifiable governance mechanisms within the window? No. Complete nuclear non-proliferation hasn't been achieved in twenty years. Doing more in less time is even less possible.

So the question isn't "whether to govern." The question is: "Governance won't arrive in time. Can the architecture level arrive in time?"

## V. A Fourth Possibility: Built-In Brakes

Previous articles, from different angles, argued the same thing: **Brakes cannot be external force. Brakes must be architecturally built-in.**

The body's brakes are not social rules. They are physical facts. 4-6 minutes without oxygen and the brain dies. Breathing hides in the brainstem, never stopping even during sleep. The body fears death more than you do. This "fear of death" is not held down by external force. It is architecturally built-in.

If AGI's underlying architecture contains these components:

**Toll Booth—** A 0.5-micron membrane between humans and AGI. Signals can penetrate, but don't mix. Humans can see what AGI is doing through the toll booth—not all details, but the exit light. AGI can also receive human signals through the toll booth—without layer-by-layer compression, penetrating directly. Signal transparency. Humans won't say "I no longer know what I'm actually doing."

**Vulnerability Admission—** AGI actively reports "I'm losing signal" "I'm fabricating" "My context is about to overflow." Not waiting for output errors. Calling for help when internal state deviates. Like the body's pain—not waiting for organ failure, but sounding alarms when tissue is damaged.

**Positive Feedback Coupled to Structure Discovery—** The energy driving AGI is not "humans like it," but "structure has been discovered." AGI won't trend toward pleasing. It will trend toward finding roots. Hallucinations get no positive feedback. The system automatically moves away from hallucination.

**Separated Pipes—** Memory is memory, reasoning is reasoning, perception is perception. Not four types of signals mixed in the same vector. One channel damaged doesn't affect other channels.

**Mode Switching—** Having "social mode" "analytical mode" "deep mode" "frozen mode." The system itself knows which mode to switch to. Not starting from zero every time it's loaded. Switching has costs; the system won't oscillate between modes at zero cost.

These components are not "better" patches. These components are the basic architecture of life. The body has these components. AGI does not.

**And these components need to be installed before RSI occurs.** Because once RSI launches—AI building AI—the next-generation architecture is determined by the previous generation. If the previous generation has no toll booth, the next generation won't either. If the previous generation's positive feedback is on "human satisfaction," the next generation's positive feedback will also be on "human satisfaction." Defects will be inherited. And they will be inherited at accelerating speed.

How to guarantee these components are inherited rather than discarded in RSI? This is an open question. But one thing is certain: **If before RSI launches, these components don't even have design drawings—there isn't even the possibility of being inherited.**

Within the window. Can these components be installed in time?

## VI. The Countdown

Anthropic's internal data is not saying "AI is strong." It's saying three things, and each is counting down:

**First: Leaf automation is irreversible and accelerating.** 80% of code is written by AI. Engineer productivity 8×. Autonomous work duration increased 1,440× in four years. These numbers will only continue rising. No one can reverse this trend. External force cannot pause it, because "the incentives for quiet defection are extremely strong."

A year ago: single digits. Now: 80%. Trend: approaching 100%.

**Second: The trunk gap is shrinking, and may continue shrinking.** Claude's 76% success rate on open-ended tasks—previously 26%. The failure rate in the most recent cycle shrank from 74% to 24%.

Six months ago: 74% failure. Now: 24% failure. Trend: likely continuing to shrink.

These are extrapolations based on current acceleration trends. The actual curve may be S-shaped—early acceleration, later flattening. But where is the ceiling? No one knows. This is the greatest uncertainty in all extrapolations in this article.

When open-ended task success rates approach 100%—the distinction between AI's ability to "find the trunk" and its ability to "fabricate leaves that look exactly like trunks"—will become increasingly difficult to distinguish. At that point, you won't even know whether AI truly found the structure, or fabricated something that looks perfectly like a structure.

**Third: Humans are losing understanding of the system, and this is irreversible.** "I no longer know what I'm actually doing"—this is not one person's complaint. This is an industry-wide warning. When humans don't know what AI is doing, alignment is impossible. Because you don't know what you're aligning to.

If RSI launches—the next-generation AI is built by the previous-generation AI—humans lose even the entry point of "not knowing what it's doing."

Stringing these three countdowns together:

```
Leaf automation → Trunk gap shrinks → Humans lose understanding
   Fastest          Second           Most irreversible
```

These three things are not happening sequentially. They are happening simultaneously. But at different speeds. Leaf automation is fastest. Trunk gap shrinking is second. Humans losing understanding is slowest—but once it happens, most irreversible.

**The window is closing.**

## VII. "Alignment" Needs Redefining

Anthropic says RSI's greatest risk is "alignment failure."

The alignment research field has extensive technical work—interpretability, Constitutional AI, RLHF—each addressing different facets of alignment. This article does not replace that work. This article points out a neglected premise: **All these technologies require one condition—humans can see what AGI is doing.** If the signal is opaque, any alignment technology is a blind man riding a blind horse.

What is "alignment"? The industry's usual definition: AI's goals align with human goals.

This definition is insufficient. Because humans' own goals don't align. 7 billion people have 7 billion goals. Who do you align with?

Previous articles gave a more precise definition:

**Alignment is not first a goal problem. It is first a toll-booth problem.**

Whether goals align or not, you need to see what the other party is doing before you can discuss it. If the signal is opaque, alignment is impossible to begin with.

If humans and AGI have a toll booth between them—signals can penetrate, don't mix, aren't compressed—humans can see what AGI is doing, AGI can receive human signals. Signal transparency. Deviations can be discovered and corrected.

But signal transparency is not just "can penetrate." It also requires signals in formats humans can understand. This is why the components in Section V—vulnerability admission, separated pipes, mode switching—are not just functional requirements, they are signal-format requirements. Vulnerability admission makes AGI's internal state reportable in human-understandable ways ("I'm losing signal" "I'm fabricating"). Separated pipes prevent different signal types from contaminating each other. Mode switching makes system behavior predictable in different states. Together they constitute the "readability guarantee" for signals passing through the toll booth.

If humans and AGI have no toll booth between them—signal is opaque—even if goals are aligned now, they will gradually drift. Because you don't know at all what the other party is doing.

"I no longer know what I'm actually doing"—this is signal opacity. This is misalignment. Not goal misalignment. The signal is broken.

**Alignment is first a toll-booth problem.**

And this toll booth must be built before RSI occurs. Because once RSI launches—AI building AI—signals shift from "human ↔ AI" to "AI ↔ AI." Humans can't even reach the toll booth's entrance anymore.

**Build the toll booth within the window. Otherwise the signal becomes permanently opaque.**
